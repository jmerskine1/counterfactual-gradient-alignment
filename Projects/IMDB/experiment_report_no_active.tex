\textbf{Active Learning:} Disabled. \\
\textbf{Variables:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Train Set Size:} \{10, 100, 200\} samples (randomly selected).
    \item \textbf{Mixing Alpha (\(\alpha\)):} Fixed at 0.5.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Observations}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Baseline Dominance:} The Cross Entropy baseline was very strong on random subsets, outperforming CGA across all sizes.
    \item \textbf{Active Learning Dependency:} The contrast with the Active Learning results (where Softplus provided a +7\% gain) highlights that CGA is most beneficial when paired with informative sample selection strategies.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
On IMDB, CGA does not provide a benefit on randomly selected datasets. The interaction between directional alignment and the representativeness of training samples appears to be a key factor in its success.
